I am an Arctic mercury biogeochemist specializing in photoredox kinetics and air–sea Hg(0) exchange. My research investigates how light, dissolved organic matter, and inorganic carbon control the redox cycling of mercury in the ocean—from contaminated estuaries to the ice-covered Arctic. I combine lab photochemistry, field cruises aboard the research vessel Araon, and multi-compartment modeling to understand mercury's fate at the ocean–atmosphere interface.

Research Interests

  • Redox Chemistry of Trace Elements in the Ocean Elucidating mechanisms of Hg redox reactions mediated by superoxide, carbonate radicals, and halogen radicals under dark and light conditions.
  • Particle Effects on Marine Biogeochemistry Investigating how particle-adsorbed metals (Mn, Fe), organic matter, and genomic composition influence marine biogeochemical processes.
  • Development of Air–Sea Flux Chambers Designing chambers that account for ocean waves and microbubbles, and comparing in-situ flux measurements with Fick's law estimates.
